How to play

A round is 9 holes, each with 1–3 balls and 1–3 sand traps. Get every ball into a hole, in as few rotations (strokes) as possible.

Choose clockwise or anticlockwise with the direction button, then use an internal rotation icon to turn its surrounding four squares.

Sand traps never move, and neither does a ball once it's sunk in its hole. A rotation is unavailable if it would carry a ball into a sand trap, or put it in an already-filled hole.

Par is a little worse than the optimal solution, so birdies, eagles, and sometimes even albatrosses are possible.

Moving to the next hole before finishing counts as a double bogey.

Scoring is modified Stableford: Albatross +8, Eagle +5, Birdie +2, Par 0, Bogey −1, Double Bogey or worse −3.
